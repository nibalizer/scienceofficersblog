HTTPS Everywhere is a browser plugin that forces your browser to use https on sites that support it. It prevents your browser from falling to ssl downgrade attacks.
I am attempting to install this on my browsers now. I use firefox and chrome daily.
Chrome version: Version 40.0.2214.95 (64-bit)
Firefox version: 35.0.1 for Ubuntu
Https everywhere, for both browsers, can be installed from the EFF's website. The EFF is an organization I trust and I actually donate to them regularly.
Chrome does not require a restart. Firefox does. Firefox brings up a dialog saying "this could break stuff, you sure you want to turn it on?" I like that.
After installing the plugin in chrome. When I go to http://xkcd.com, I end up on https://xkcd.com. When I manually change it to http, I again end up on https://. Interestingly the New York Times is being viewed via http and I can't force it to https.
Overall, https everywhere has not broken any of my usual websites, and has made me safer from ssl downgrade attacks. I am pleased with it and reccomend it to you. You can download it here.
